The Writers'Lobby

All you fans of The Jungle Book, we have an interesting piece of trivia for you. Rudyard Kipling, the author of the famous book which is loved by kids and adults alike actually spent the first 6 years of his life in Mumbai and there’s a house right here that marks his legacy.



Importance

It is said that Sir Rudyard Kipling, author of The Jungle Book, was born in 1865.

History

The Kipling House was built in 1882 and named after Rudyard Kipling. In his poem, ‘To the City of Bombay’, Kipling described the location of the bungalow. It was built more than a decade after Rudyard and his sister moved to Englsih in 1865. The original house that Kipling was born in was demolished and the Kipling House was built in the spot next to it. His father, John Lockwood, was a professor at JJ College and served as the first dean of the college. He and his wife Alice resided in the bungalow. The Kipling House was a place of residence for several college deans until the early 2000s.

Restoration Work

The conservation project began in February 2020 by the Directorate of Archaeology and Museums, a state government department. However, after beginning the structural work, funding ran out. An estimate of Rs 3.5 crore for the remaining work is being prepared to be submitted to the Secretary of Culture of Maharashtra next week. The remaining work is anticipated to take another four to six months once the funding is approved.

Victorian Architecture

The bungalow consists of four rooms, two balconies/porches on the first floor, and two large rooms along with a caretaker unit on the ground floor.
